About

Gabriel Mukobi is a web and software developer always looking to hone his skills; a musician who plays guitar and French horn in the band Simply 8 and in the Camas High School Band; a teacher of guitar, ukulele, piano, and music theory; a student at Camas High School; a player on the CHS basketball team; and an active volunteer seeking to make a difference. He is committed to doing things the correct, efficient, and elegant way.

MST Magnet

Gabe is a member of the Camas High School MST Magnet Program, a selective-admissions independent-research-based program focused on exploring math, science, and technology. He has conducted research projects to try to find solutions to a diverse set of real-world problems ranging from water purity to carbon dioxide sequestration. Gabe has competed with these projects in science competitions like the Washington State Science and Engineering Fair and Imagine Tomorrow. His projects include:

  • Measuring the Effect of Symbiotic Relationships Between Various Arbuscular Mycorrhizal Fungi and Lactuca sativa on Carbon Dioxide Uptake and Sequestration (Paper, Poster, Pictures)
  • Implementing Heat Conductive Riffles to Lessen Small Scale Thermal Pollution in Developing Nations (Paper, Poster, Designs, Cost Analysis ,)
  • The Measured Efficacy of Water Purification by a Graphene Sand Composite Filter (Paper, Poster 1, Poster 2)
